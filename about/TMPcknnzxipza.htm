About your Webmaster
Hello everybody! Thanks for visiting my website; one of the last active Space Quest fansites out there. Let me introduce myself. My name is Frans van Hofwegen (20 years old). I live in the fine, but boring, town of Nijmegen in the Netherlands close to the German borders. Let me tell you how I bummed into Sierra and their great games.

The Early Days
My dad bought our first computer (a 80286 with an ega card and colour monitor) back in 1988. My three brothers and I spent many hours on this machine, doing nothing more than staring at the screen and pushing those buttons. I really liked one particular game that came with the computer namely "Space Quest Chapter I: The Sarien Encounter". Back then, I was five years old and didn't understand much English. It was my two older brothers who helped Roger through his first adventure and it took them months. But I was right there watching their progress on the Arcada. I didn't understood anything of the texts but the pictures sure looked neat. I wanted to play my own Space Quest, so whenever the computer was free (rare occasion) I would enter the same words as my brothers did, with a dictionary as my only help.

Two years later (1990 I guess) we got a copy of Space Quest 3, my favorite Space Quest (I consider it to be the best computer game ever made). Space Quest 3 was a great challenge for my brother Tijn and his friends Guido and Daan. It was sure great to watch them play. I remember the first time I saw my favorite character Fester Blatz and his spiffy shop World O' Wonders. Once again, I was intrigued by the great science fiction graphics. Of course, I continued to learn more and more English. I just had to know why they where laughing. My next Space Quest (does this guy never shut up?) was Space Quest 5. Sadly, I didn't play that one myself because it was removed because of some computer virus. Too bad! My brothers lost interest in Space Quest as the years came and went... But I didn't...

It must be somewhere around 1996 when I stumbled across Space Quest 2 on a computer at school. I had played it for a few times only before the computer was removed.

Again some years later, my dad realized it was time for a new computer. In those days, everybody already had CD-Rom, soundcards and even internet! Even I couldn't impress my friends anymore with our trusty old 80286. I was still playing Space Quest 1 over and over again. I think I must have played it over a thousand times, really! Anyway, our second computer was a fine Pentium 200 MMX. And sweet mother of God, look at the size of that screen! It must be at least 17"! A few weeks after that, Pieter (my oldest brother) came home with.... * Drum role please * .... The Space Quest Collection! It cost me 45 bucks to buy it from him, but it was worth the money. All the Space Quest games in one box and Space Quest 7 was going to be released in 1998!

 

The Dawn of SpaceQuest.Net
Pieter and I both got into HTML and using Fortuncity (a free website host) and he asked me the other day: "Waarom maak je geen website over Space Quest?". For our non-Dutch visitors (nobody's perfect): "Why don't you make a website about Space Quest?" I replied: "GREAT IDEA!". So the next few months I was working day and night on that site. I started "The Frans and Bart Page" along with Bart Stadelmann (a friend and classmate at the time). The first version appeared online somewhere in the winter months of 1998 (probably late October). Our website featured two chapters: Space Quest and Gabriel Knight. Our favorite Sierra games. The website was extremely crappy, made with the cheap HTML-editor Frontpage2000. That site was screaming: "I'm made by highschool students without HTML knowledge, I look crappy!" A few months later, the site was hacked by a computer nerd in our class. Not soon afterwards, we (Bart and me) went our own seperate ways. His site died out rather quickly (although still online to this date). He couldn't push himself to re-create all that was lost (we didn't do no pussy back-ups back then). I almost stopped too, but I got around re-doing a lot of work and put together a new version.

The pictures you see here are one of the first images I did for the site, and the only two to survive the hack. A funny note: The Space Quest 3 Fanfiction page about Monolith Burger was one of the first pages I made. It's still online to this date.

In July 2000, I was in the middle of redesigning the looks of the website using the Trellix webeditor for Fortunecities when the update craze came to a sudden stop: putting a halt to 1.5 years of hard work on the site. The main reason why I stopped updating was the lack of visitors I was getting, the fact Space Quest 7 was cancelled months earlier (on February the 22nd, 1999) and, most notably, because I had overwritten a HUGE chunk of work on my harddisk by accident. I just couldn't make myself do get work done.

 

Growing and Expanding
Almost a year later (in June 2001) I had this urge to revive the site (after not having touched it for a whole year). I invested almost all of my free summer days making a new design. I decided Trellix and Fortunecity wasn't going to cut it, so I used Dreamwaver 4 and a Geocities website instead (click on the picture to the left for a screenshot of the July 2001 to March 2002 version). Some weeks later, school term started, but I continued to invest huge amounts of time in the website. All the hard work was not in vain, as the amount of visitors increased tremendously from 105 per month by June 2001, to 699 per month by July 2001. The site began to crossing the three gigabyte datalimit from Geocities, causing the entire site to be shut down. Asthonished by the fact my site was getting popular so quickly, I was thinking about the possibility of having my own domainname. Geocities was just starting to offer "professional packages" which included a domainname, extra server space and more bandwidth. All for a very reasonable price. Another advantage was an ad-free site, and it could still be accessed using the geocities address. It was just what I needed. The domainname was bought on the 17th of September 2001. I decided to go for that particular name because .com, .org and http://www.wiw.org/~jess/roger.html were already taken. A funny note: I was owner of spacequest.nl for a very short time, but I cancelled it because I thought the name spacequest.net had more future.

Inspired by a previous DarkMark design, I released a new design on March 9, 2002. The same date marked the name change of the site from "Frans' Space Quest Page" (or was it Site?) to "SpaceQuest.Net - Slippery When Wet!". This design took the website to an even higher level. I was getting over 5000 visitors a month at the end of its lifetime. Quite an achievement, don't you agree? I'm still very attached to the simplicity of this design, but it had served for over 18 months. It was time to scrap it and look ahead.

When reached its 5th Anniversary - at the October 25th, 2003 - a new design was introduced. Fellow Space Quest fan Danny Bloks worked his ass of during a four month period and got up with a terrific design. We introduced several truly unique features. Think about the neat pop up windows and online QuickTime video's. But also content-wise, like the awesome design sketches from the cancelled SQ7. We definitely made sure was ready for the future.

 

The Near Future
From Fortunecity to Geocities to Featureprice to Eperfect.net to Lavahost to AffordableHost to SQ7.Org's private server: has seen it all and has been around longer than most websites our there. And I can safely say the last updates aren't insight yet... This is where the story ends... For now.
