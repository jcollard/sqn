>> Miscellaneous >> Buying the Games

I used to get weekly mails from people who were desperately trying to find a copy of their favorite game: Space Quest. I've therefore written this article which helps you to find, and eventually buy, your Space Quest game.

Abandoning Space Quest

The last Space Quest item Sierra sold was the Space Quest Collection, which contained all the games from the Series. This particular item wasn't all that wide-spread (which wasn't the case with previous Space Quest products), but at least it was available at their website. Sadly, they stopped selling that package in 1998. To make matters worse, they also stopped offering technical support, game hints and replacement manuals. Simply put: they abandoned the games.

At the moment, there's no reason to believe they are ever going to sell the games again. If they are ever to resume production (fat chance), Amazon.com is the first place to check as they handle Sierra's online sales. The collection (although out of stock and discontinued) is set to $20 US. Sierra has mentioned it more than once: Putting the Space Quest Collection back into production is too expensive. They gave two reasons:

The high royalties attached. Royalties means that certain people (I.E. the guys who created Space Quest) receive a cut from each copy sold. At the time Space Quest was hot stuff, it wasn't unusable for the designers to receive 5% royalties per copy. These agreements made between the designers and Sierra still stand (even though they might have been sacked or left to work elsewhere).

The collection will not sell enough copies to make a profit out of it. Let's assume for a moment that Sierra needs to order 25,000 units from their suppliers to make selling the collection economical feasable. And let's say they're going to sell 500 copies a month, which is pretty optimistic if you ask me. This means it'll take more than 4 years until every copy is sold! Can you image how much they'll have to spend on storage, for instance?

We've reached a dead end, right? If Sierra can't help us, who can?

 

Buying the Games - Bagain Bins

The Space Quest games are very hard to find, but it's not impossible. The first thing you might want to check are the bargain bins of your local software store. You never know what's hidden inside these bins'o'pleasure. I've bought several Space Quest items this way, including hintbooks. I must add though: the more time passes, the more unlikely it is to find Space Quest in a bargain bin. However, I've found a copy of Space Quest 6 as recently as in 2002.

 

Buying the Games - used Amazon items

Space Quest is just one of the many products offered at Amazon.com. Amazon happens to be Sierra official online seller. However, the collection is out of stock and discontinued. From time to time though, copies of the Series (not limited to the collection) are offered by Amazon users. Be a bit careful not to spend too much.

 

Buying the Games - eBay Auctions

I always recommend people to eBay, the largest online auction website in the world. It's your the best option if to buy Space Quest. Go to the website and search for "Space Quest". You'll get presented with a list of games that people are selling at that time. This means that if you can't find your Space Quest, don't worry! Return some days later and try again. Their might be someone else now who's selling the sequel you're looking for.

The games are offered for dirty cheap prices most of the time. It isn't very difficult getting your hands on Space Quest 3 for $7.50 US for instance. On the other hand, the collection packages are quite frequently sold for well over $100 US bucks. I therefore recommend buying the games seperately, unless you live overseas and have to pay for the shipping costs, which can be quite high (probably around $10 to $15 US). Many sellers are willing to ship to your country, eventhough they live oversea. Contact them and ask for the shipping costs just to be sure.

You need your own account before you can start bidding. Signing up for an account is free. One of the most frequent questions I hear from people is: Can you trust sellers? Well, you can never be 100% certain. And the seller might not be problem: your package might be lost during shipping for instance. So how can you be sure, or relatively sure? I recommend bidding on sellers who have over 20 postive feedbacks. It's highly unlikely that a person with a high amount of positive feedback is about to scam you. Mind you, all my transactions at Ebay went great, and I've done quite a few. I've bought my entire Space Quest Collection at Ebay, and that's saying something. Not one trade went unsatisfactory! But how are you going to pay the seller? I recommend using PayPal. You can register for an account which enables you to pay for auctions, as well as other things, by means of your credit card. Don't worry about safety: You'll only have to enter your credit card data once, and you can use your account for free (when paying) over and over again. Please note that the seller at Ebay must accept paypal. But a lot do so.
