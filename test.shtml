Welcome to SpaceQuest.Net!

Welcome to SpaceQuest.Net! This website pays tribute to Space Quest: the wacky sci-fi humor adventure computer game series by Sierra On-Line, starring space janitor Roger Wilco! SpaceQuest.Net is the ultimate Space Quest resource website, containing everything you wanted to know about Space Quest, and then some. Put on your Astro Chicken flight hat and enjoy the website!

What is so special about SpaceQuest.Net?

This website goes far beyond your regular fansite. Of course it offers screen shots, game information and technical support on all six games. But it goes a lot deeper than that. It offers bucket loads of unique and exclusive information about the Space Quest Series. By talking directly to people who helped creating the Series (people like Shawn Sharp, Mark Crowe, Richard Powell, Ken Williams, Tim Loucks, William Shockley, Al Lowe, Josh Mandel and many others) this website is able to offer huge amounts of "behind the scenes information" but also a great number of the original design sketches and even some design documents. This is the only place on the internet where you can find all five of the official hint books. The manuals have become rare items these days. There are only a few copies out there. That's why this website offers you high resolution scans of the original documentation of every Space Quest game. You'll also find high resolution scans of the Roger Wilco Comics, three ultra rare comic books released in a very limited print run in the early 1990's. And let's not forget about a number of unique SQ video's from old promotional VHS tapes!

These are just a few things of what I call "SQN Exclusives". Remember, other websites can only copy this exclusive content: SpaceQuest.Net brought it to you first. With pride.

SpaceQuest.Net is hosting a message board called the Janitorial Times. If you've been hanging around the net since 1997 or so, you might remember this name from the first official Space Quest forums on the Sierra website. Sadly, Sierra On-Line and her forums are no more. Fans only have themselves to turn to for technical support and game help. That's why this website tries to keep the Space Quest Community alive. In tradition of the old Sierra forums, the message board on this website is freely accessible and available to all. It doesn't matter if you've just learned about the Space Quest games, or if you are a weathered veteran. I personally invite you to join one of the oldest computer gaming communities on the internet. Kindred spirits can be found at the best place to discuss Space Quest and you are more than welcome to join us!

This website wasn't build in one day. In fact, it's over 6 years old (which is ancient for a website!). If you want to know how this website came to life, then please visit the about page.
